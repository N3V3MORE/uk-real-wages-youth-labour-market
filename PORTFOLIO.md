# Real Wages and Youth Labour Market Stress in the UK, 2019-2026

Built a reproducible Python and Streamlit project analysing whether UK workers, especially younger workers, became better or worse off after inflation since 2019. The project uses official ASHE, CPIH, PAYE RTI, A05, EARN01, and minimum wage data, with a robustness harness, source-value audit, and reviewer guide. The main finding is deliberately cautious: the 18-21 ASHE weekly-earnings result is fragile, RTI 18-24 monthly PAYE pay points in a different direction, and ASHE decomposition shows rising hourly pay but falling paid hours.
